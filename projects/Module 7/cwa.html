Charts Worksheet and Analysis

Compulsory e-Portfolio Component

Complete the Charts Worksheet and Analysis exercise. This worksheet demonstrates how different chart types and visualisation choices impact data interpretation and understanding. The exercise explores percentage frequency bar charts, relative frequency histograms, and other graphical representations, highlighting how class intervals, bin widths, and scaling choices influence the interpretation of data distributions.

Key Learning Points

Data Presentation is Not Neutral

This worksheet reinforced a central learning point: data presentation is not neutral. Even statistically sound analyses can mislead if visual design exaggerates differences or removes context. The exercise demonstrated how percentage frequency bar charts and relative frequency histograms enable meaningful comparisons, but outcomes remain sensitive to class intervals, bin widths, and scaling choices.

Impact of Visualisation Choices

For example, when comparing Diet A and Diet B distributions, Diet A appeared more consistent than Diet B, yet this conclusion depends on how distributions are constructed and displayed. Different bin widths or class intervals can dramatically alter the visual impression of data consistency and variation.

Ethical Considerations in Data Visualisation

The worksheet highlighted the ethical risks of misleading graphical presentation. Inappropriate chart choices, distorted scales, and selective emphasis can obscure meaning and bias interpretation. This aligns with Berenson et al. (2015), who emphasise that charts shape interpretation as much as they report results.

Application to Research Practice

These insights inform how visual choices will be justified in research proposals and dissertations. Well-structured visualisations support clarity when appropriately matched to the data and research question, while poorly chosen visualisations can mislead readers and undermine research validity.
