Part 1: Coursework: Group Report

Internet Musician, DJ, aspiring producer and YouTube influencer DJ Ascentia (current followers 1M) has been approached by two companies to endorse and promote their new products.

ARMband Product

The first product from Band of Bros is called ARMband – marketed as a "Band in a Box" it is based on the successful, Raspberry-Pi based Zynthian open-source product (Rogerson, 2023), but it has its own branded Sequencer, Sampler and Instruments forked from open-source alternatives. Its USP is a new 4-in, 4 -out AD/ DA card that makes the instrument much more flexible – however this board does not currently have Linux compatible drivers. The company has a small but dedicated development team that are tasked with the UI rebranding. Other software developments (such as the new driver) will need to be developed by the community. The owners of the Armband product are intending to differentiate themselves from their obvious competitor (i.e. Zynthian) in two ways: first by offering a fully built, tested and warrantied product at a lower price than the Zynthian kit due to a cheaper case and simpler build (ARMband will have a recommended retail price (RRP) of £400). Secondly, the development team have plans to license and port a number of shareware applications (known as Virtual Studio Technology (VST) effects and instruments) to their platform to further differentiate their product from the competition. The company require DJ Ascentia to invest £10,000 towards the case tooling and manufacture, and they are offering a 15% share of the profits as payback.

Runi Product

The second product is known as Runi (Risc-V Unix-native instrument) from a company called The Runes. Runi is based on the idea of Norns from Monome. The Norns is based around community contributed scripts written in a language called Lua, and the Supercollider engine (Rothwell, 2020). Unlike the Norns product, Runi is designed to be based on a RISC-V development board (The Vision5 from Starfive). The development board is relatively new, and it does not have the wide level of software support of common ARM boards – for example, Supercollider and the Lua engine need to be ported and the sound driver needs some development work. The team behind Runi is a 2-person unit, and while one is a software developer, they have decided to use common open-source community development resources to help them develop their product. They have also decided to use mostly commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) components for their product. The only exception is the case which they have decided will be a 3-D printed component. The unique selling points (USPs) of the Runi over the standard Monome Norns is the price, which will be £150 for a self-assembly kit (consisting of the case, documentation/ instructions, and a screen – the purchaser will supply their own RISC-V board) or £400 for a fully built, tested and warrantied unit. The other advantage will be a standardised user interface (UI) to make it easier to use and quicker to learn. The team have decided to develop the UI in-house, but will need the community to help develop and test the drivers for the hardware as well as help with porting the Supercollider engine, the Lua interpreter, and the scripts from the original Norns. The Runes team are not asking for investment, just product placement, promotion and marketing and in return they are offering a 7% share of the gross profit.

Both companies will only accept an exclusive deal (i.e. they will not accept DJ Ascentia pursuing both options). DJ Ascentia would like to start promoting the most lucrative option as soon as possible, to maximise his income/ earnings. His management company have recruited your company to produce independent plans and estimates for both products. Which option would you recommend that the influencer should take?

Your Group Report Requirements:

You need to extract the pertinent requirements from the description given above. In addition, you are required to recommend the best project management methodology for each project, with appropriate justifications for each. You should enumerate the 5 key requirements for each product in the form of gherkin statements.

You are also tasked with creating a WBS/Project Network diagram that enumerates the "work packages" that are required by each project. You are also required to create a detailed project plan (using a Gantt Chart) that shows how the work packages would run in parallel, and the duration of each package.

Do not forget to include work packages for software development and the various testing stages – including unit, integration, and user acceptance. Remember that even "free" packages (for example development or testing provided by community resources) still takes time and must be included to provide a fair comparison in the elapsed time calculations.

You are required to estimate the time required for software development (including porting), testing, and any required documentation. You should justify the estimates you have made. Bear in mind every package you created for the WBS needs an estimate and should appear on your plan. You also need to create an estimate of the total cost to bring these products to market (excluding advertising and promotion which is what DJ Ascentia is needed for!).

You need to ensure that your report is carefully formatted and checked to a level suitable for a business presentation.

You also need to ensure that you have included suitable references, and that all the illustrations and figures you have included are appropriately cited.

Academic Integrity and Plagiarism

We take academic integrity very seriously. Academic integrity means acting with fairness and honesty, giving credit to others where you are referring to their ideas or research and respecting the work of others. Plagiarism is defined as: 'Using or copying the work of others (whether written, printed or in any other form) without proper acknowledgement'. Before you finalise your assignment take time to check that all your statements are backed up with supporting evidence, that all sources you use - whether referring to their ideas, quoting directly or paraphrasing - are correctly referenced in the text. Correct use of referencing acknowledges the academic whose work has informed yours, enables the reader to find the sources you have used and demonstrates your ability to find and analyse relevant information.

Failure to properly acknowledge the work of others is an academic offence and may result in your work incurring a penalty or, in the most serious cases, you being removed from the course for academic dishonesty.

If you are unsure about referencing or plagiarism there are useful resources available in the Study Skills Hub which is accessible from the menu on the left-hand side. If you are still experiencing difficulties with academic integrity then you can contact the Study Skills Team for individualised support.
