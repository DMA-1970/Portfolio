This is an individual submission. The word count is 1,500 words equivalent/20 minutes.

Brief

ARMband Update

ARMband have seen how successful DJ Ascentia influencing/marketing is and have decided to change their offer to the influencer. They no longer require an investment and are offering an 8% share of the gross profit. Additionally, they are happy to accept a non-exclusive deal with the influencer. The ARMband is a fully working and tested system, but so far the company has only sold 750 systems in the first year.

Runi Challenges

The Runes, however, are less happy. The actual sales of their Runi kit have only been 1750 rather than the predicted 2750. They have not sold any of the complete systems. This is down to several factors:

Firstly, the porting of the Norns software to the Runi has not gone well. As part of the process it transpires that one of the UI components is NOT open source and there is no design for it. The Runes engineers are working to reverse-engineer the component, but until this is complete a working system will not be available. The estimate for the development of this fix is 6 weeks (excluding testing).

Secondly, and because of the above, DJ Ascentia has not been able to create any working demos of the Runi – which has also affected sales.

Thirdly, because Runi is Risc-V based, there is no other alternative software available that can use the hardware – limiting its usefulness without the ported software.

The influencer reckons that it will take him 4 weeks to create the demos once the fix has been applied.

Additionally, there is a 50% chance that the Runes will withdraw their business if the influencer takes on the ARMband. The Band of Bros have said if this happens they will increase the influencer's payment to 11% of gross profit.

Academic Integrity and Plagiarism

We take academic integrity very seriously. Academic integrity means acting with fairness and honesty, giving credit to others where you are referring to their ideas or research and respecting the work of others. Plagiarism is defined as: 'Using or copying the work of others (whether written, printed or in any other form) without proper acknowledgement'. Before you finalise your assignment take time to check that all your statements are backed up with supporting evidence, that all sources you use - whether referring to their ideas, quoting directly or paraphrasing - are correctly referenced in the text. Correct use of referencing acknowledges the academic whose work has informed yours, enables the reader to find the sources you have used and demonstrates your ability to find and analyse relevant information.

Failure to properly acknowledge the work of others is an academic offence and may result in your work incurring a penalty or, in the most serious cases, you being removed from the course for academic dishonesty.

If you are unsure about referencing or plagiarism there are useful resources available in the Study Skills Hub which is accessible from the menu on the left hand side. If you are still experiencing difficulties with academic integrity then you can contact the Study Skills Team for individualised support.
